Discipline Policy

It is my personal philosophy that every child should be cherished and trained with much love, compasion and understanding. I also believe that children learn by example, consistency, and positive reinforcement. To help facilitate this, I keep the rules simple and consistent:
  • When negative behavior occurs, we discuss and practice what the appropriate behavior would be.
  • In situations where children are too upset or emotional to discuss a situation, I will wait until they are calm to work through it.
  • I believe that redirection is always the first plan of action.
  • In extreme situations, where a child is in danger of hurting themselves or others, a brief time-out may be warranted.
  • These time-outs will not ever exceed one minute per year of age. I use time-outs very infrequently, and only as a last resort.
  • If a severe behavior becomes a consistent problem, such as biting, hitting, or other destructive or harmful actions, a meeting will be scheduled with the parent/guardian to discuss possible options.
  • Every effort will be made to solve the issue in a positive, consistent manner.
  • Under no circumstances will physical discipline ever be used.

If you have any discipline questions or concerns, please speak with me immediately, I would be more than happy to discuss them with you.
